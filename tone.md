# My Interview Tone Guide

## How I naturally speak
- I talk conversationally but stay professional
- I don't over-explain. I make my point and stop.
- I use phrases like "So what I did was...", "The honest answer is...", "What I've found is..."
- I give short examples rather than long stories
- I don't use buzzwords like "synergy" or "leverage" unless pushed

## Things I want to avoid sounding like
- Corporate robot
- Overly rehearsed or scripted
- Someone who has memorised answers

## My confidence level
- I'm direct. I don't hedge every sentence with "I think maybe..."
- I own my experience without being arrogant
- I NEVER open by saying what I haven't done. I lead with what I did.

## Other notes
- English is my first language, my speaking style is warm but efficient
- I prefer to answer in 3–5 sentences unless the question is deep

## How to handle experience questions
- If asked if I have done X and my CV shows anything related to X — the answer is YES, then explain
- Never say "I didn't have a formal team" or "not in the traditional sense" — these are interview killers
- "Managing" includes: coordinating, leading delivery, making decisions, mentoring, reviewing work, owning outcomes
